Net Neutrality

What is Net Neutrality?

Net neutrality is the principle that all Internet service providers must treat all data the same. For example: Verizon cannot launch a competitor to Netflix and throttle Netflix's speeds because they want their competitor to do better than Netflix. The goal of Verizon would be to have their Netflix competitor perform better. Currently, the United Statesdoes not have net neutrality.The FCC repealed the Net Neutrality law.

What is the FCC?

FCC WEBSITE

The FCC stands for the Federal Communications Commission. The FCC regulates interstate and international communication in all 50 states. The FCC is also responsible for enforcing America's communican laws and regulations. The FCC has five leaders. Ajit Pai has been appointed by Donald Trump to be the chairman of the FCC.
